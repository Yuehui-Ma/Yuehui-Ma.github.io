Yuehui Ma 马月辉
Visiting Researcher
Purple Mountain Observatory, Chinese Academy of Sciences, Nanjing, China

I am currently a Visiting Researcher at the Purple Mountain Observatory, Chinese Academy of Sciences. My research focuses on the physics of the Galactic interstellar medium, particularly the structure, dynamics, and turbulence of molecular clouds, and their role in star formation.

My work combines large-scale CO and HI surveys, including the MWISP project, with statistical analyses of molecular cloud structure and kinematics. I am interested in turbulent cascades across multiple spatial scales, hierarchical cloud structures, scaling relations, and the interplay between turbulence, gravity, and Galactic environment. I aim to better understand how turbulence regulates the evolution of molecular clouds and the formation of stars in the Milky Way.
